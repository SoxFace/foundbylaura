Street Finds

My name is Laura and I am a collector. I have 12 years worth of notes that I have found on the ground. From love letters, angry parking notes, funny and strange shopping lists to passive aggressive flat mate notes. They are beautiful, painful, funny, daring and everything that we humans are. Enjoy.

About Laura

I grew up in suburban Adelaide, Australia. Ever since i can remember i was picking up junk off the ground. Shiny things, odd objects and even collected a good amount of money. I guess i’ve always looked down.

One day back in February 2005 i was going about my day doing my usual grocery shop at my local Woolworths when i found a bright orange sticky note on the shop floor. Just like my child self i was intrigued by the orange note with the scribble of handwriting so i picked it up. it read, ‘bbq chicken, bread, milk, muffins..: and on the other side it read “Andrew - drug and alcohol appt 1st Feb” I was immediately moved by the fact that i’d peeked into a personal side of someones life. How many people had walked over it, how many trolls run over it? The thought of this very intimate part of someones life being discarded on the ground fascinated me. So i kept it. Just on my self at home. Then one day on my work lunch break i found a note written on an old receipt that said “i love you BeBe, your angel” Of course i picked that up, and kept it. Then i found a few more great ones. One day i looked that that little pile of notes on my self and i thought to myself, i think this is now a thing.

Over the years i’ve lived i New York, Toronto, Melbourne and Sydney. The journey of my travels can be seen through my found notes.

I’m currently living in Sydney and continue to be amazed that there are still great finds out there just waiting to be found.
